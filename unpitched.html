Literally Everything Else

Unpitched Percussion is the single largest instrument group period. The thing about unpitched percussion is that it can be just about anything! Drumset, conga drums, Tam-Tams, tom-toms, bass drums, sanre drums, cajons, tambourines, waterphones, wad-of-paper-in-bowls, the possibilities are endless because one can find a way to hit just about anything and make it musical! Even pitched instruments can beome non-pitched by hitting tthe frames or taking off individual bars and playing them individually. Many composers in the modern era are really beginning to push the boundaries of percussive instrument in ways that are very interseting to behold. There are many pieces where percussionists are instructed to hit bags of silverware and soup cans to create a certain sound. It is just as interesting as it is hilarious.
